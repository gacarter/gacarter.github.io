I am a PhD student in the CDT in NLP at The University of Edinburgh, supervised by Dr. Paul Hoffman and Dr. Frank Keller. My research interests lie in the intersection of Cognitive Neuroscience and NLP. I am exploring the influences of context on semantic representations through the use of behavioural and computational methods. 

Previously, I completed an MSc in Cognitive Neuroscience with a specialisation in Language & Communication from Radboud University, The Netherlands in conjunction with the Donders Institute for Brain, Cognition and Behaviour. My MSc thesis focused on the influence of definiteness on predictive processing and was supervised by Dr. Mante Nieuwland at the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics.
